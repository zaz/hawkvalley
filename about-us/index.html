More about the Company: Hawk Valley Productions

Hawk Valley Productions is a small production group based in rural Northumberland. We largely work with DSLR media and create  film productions and photographic projects through the year.

In our projects we draw inspiration from a range of sources, artists, and directors - but always trying to remain original.

Founded by Jem Freiesleben  in 2009, Hawk Valley Productions is not a commercial enterprise, our projects are available for free online, and elsewhere.

Current Group Members



Jem Freiesleben

Jem Freiesleben is the founder of the group. He oversees many aspects of the projects, working as the Script-Writer in pre-production, Director, Actor, and Camera during production, and Editor for post-production.

“As the group director I oversee aspects of practically all productions made in the group. I am particularly interested in ‘futuristic’ themes, which include Science Fiction and Dystopian. These themes are both to feature heavily in our two upcoming productions: “Hello World” and “Credit Roll”. I have edited and directed many of the recent productions in the group.”

At GCSE he achieved 4 A*– grades, 7 A – grades, and one B – grade, including A* at GCSE art. Jem has also gained several awards in film and photography.

Crispin Agar

Crispin joined the company almost immediately after formation in 2009. He has worked on the majority of productions since. Roles include: Actor, Co-Director, and Camera. He wrote the script for the 2012 production short, “Scarecrow” which was screened at the AMC Cinema Manchester, as part of the Co-Operative Film festival.

Crispin won first prize (working independently) in the prestigious Port of Tyne Awards, 2011, for his short film.

 

Elliott Wallis 

Elliott joined the group in 2010 has worked as an Actor and as Cameraman in both the entries to the Co-Operative film festival: "Chemical Dust" (2011 - Bradford) and "Scarecrow" (2012 - Manchester).

Elliott often works with other professional groups including theatre, and has an acting role in the BBC production "Wolfblood".

 

George Cooper 

Joining Hawk Valley Productions with its first Northumberland Production, Seven Stones (2009), George mainly works as Co-Director and Editor within the group. He also works with camera, and as script editor. 


A note on Sustainability:

We believe sustainability we always try to minimise any environmental impact of our projects by using recycled or recyclable materials such as timber.

Rather than buying special equipment which could only see a use on one project, (such as custom camera tracks) we manually construct it from suitable sustainable or recycled materials wherever possible.  Although this often takes more time, or comes at a higher cost, anything that lowers environmental impact of any project large or small is ultimately better.
